Atlanta, Ga

Atlanta is the capital of the U.S. state of Georgia. It played an important part in both the Civil War and the 1960s Civil Rights Movement. Atlanta History Center chronicles the city's past, and the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site is dedicated to the African-American leader’s life and times. Downtown, Centennial Olympic Park, built for the 1996 Olympics, encompasses the massive Georgia Aquarium.Click here for a guide

Georgia Aquarium

The Georgia Aquarium is the only Aquarium in the world to house the largest fish, the whale shark.

Major Corporations

From the most recognized soda brand to the largest home improvement retailer in the world, Atlanta is the origin of companies like The Coca-Cola Company, UPS, Delta Air Lines, The Home Depot and Chick-fil-A.

Centennial Olympic Park

Located at the center of downtown Atlanta is Georgia’s historical legacy of the 1996 Summer Olympic Games. The landmark is decorated with sculptures, spacious greenery, gardens and the one-of-a-kind Olympic Ring Fountain.
